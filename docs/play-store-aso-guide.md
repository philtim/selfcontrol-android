### 1.4 Full Description — Version B (Policy-Safe, Ultra-Clear)

#### English

```
FocusTime is a commitment enforcement tool for Android. It blocks selected apps and websites during timed focus sessions, designed for developers, writers, researchers, and anyone whose work requires sustained concentration.

Unlike gentle screen time reminders, FocusTime enforces the boundaries you set. Once a session starts, blocked apps and websites remain inaccessible until the timer expires.


WHAT FOCUSTIME DOES

• App Blocking — Select apps to block from all installed apps or from a list of your most-used apps. During a session, opening a blocked app triggers a full-screen blocking overlay that redirects you to your home screen.

• Website Blocking — Add domains to your blocklist (e.g. instagram.com) or use wildcard patterns (e.g. *.youtube.com) to block a domain and all subdomains. Websites are filtered via local DNS interception through a device-local VPN service. No network traffic is routed through external servers.

• Timed Sessions — Choose a preset duration (30 minutes to 8 hours) or set a custom timer. A countdown is shown on the home screen and in the notification bar. Sessions remain active after device reboots.

• Accountability Password — Optionally set a master password. When set, the password is required to end a session before the timer expires. This feature is designed for accountability — share the password with someone you trust so you cannot override your own commitment.

• Session Completion — When the timer finishes, all blocks are removed automatically. A summary screen shows your total focused time.

FocusTime contains no gamification, no streaks, no rewards, and no engagement mechanics. It does one thing: enforce the focus boundaries you define.


HOW IT WORKS

1. Open FocusTime and add apps or websites to your blocklist.
2. Select a duration for your focus session.
3. Optionally set a master password and share it with an accountability partner.
4. Tap "Start Focus." Blocking services activate immediately.
5. If you open a blocked app, a blocking screen appears with a "Return Home" button. If you visit a blocked website, the DNS request is filtered and the site does not load.
6. When the session ends, all apps and websites are unblocked automatically.

If a master password is set, tapping "Emergency Override" on the blocking screen prompts for the password. This is the only way to end a session early.


PRIVACY

• All data is stored locally on your device.
• No user account is required. No registration. No login.
• No analytics or tracking libraries are included.
• No personal data is collected, transmitted, or shared.
• The local VPN service intercepts DNS requests on your device only. It does not route traffic through external servers and does not log browsing activity.
• Passwords are stored as secure one-way hashes on the device.


PERMISSIONS EXPLAINED

FocusTime requires the following permissions to function:

• Usage Access — Required to detect which app is currently in the foreground, enabling app blocking.
• VPN — Required to create a local VPN service that filters DNS requests on the device for website blocking. No traffic is sent to external servers.
• Display Over Other Apps — Required to show the blocking overlay when a blocked app is opened.
• Device Admin — Used to protect the active focus session from being interrupted.
• Notifications — Required to display the active session countdown notification.
• Boot Completed — Required to restart blocking services if the device reboots during a session.

Each permission is requested at runtime when the relevant feature is first used. Each is explained before being requested.


REQUIREMENTS

• Android 8.0 (API 26) or higher.

FocusTime is a focused, single-purpose tool for people who take deep work seriously.
```

## 3. Policy & Claim Safety Check

### 3.1 Risky Phrases & Compliant Alternatives

| # | Risky Phrase / Claim | Risk | Compliant Alternative |
|---|---------------------|------|----------------------|
| 1 | "Cannot be uninstalled" | Misleading; Android always allows uninstall via settings | "Helps protect the active session from being interrupted" |
| 2 | "Uninstall protection" | Implies blocking system-level behavior; policy risk | "Session protection via Device Admin" |
| 3 | "Prevents you from using apps" | Overpromises; user can always force-stop services | "Shows a blocking screen when you open a blocked app" |
| 4 | "Impossible to bypass" | Unverifiable; user can always revoke permissions | "Designed to keep you accountable" |
| 5 | "Locks your phone" | Suggests device locking, not app blocking | "Blocks selected apps during your focus session" |
| 6 | "VPN" (without context) | May trigger policy review; must clarify local-only | "Local-only VPN for on-device DNS filtering" |
| 7 | "Monitors your apps" | Sounds like surveillance | "Detects when a blocked app is opened" |
| 8 | "Tracks your usage" | Sounds like data collection | "Checks which app is in the foreground" |
| 9 | "Addiction cure" / "Cures phone addiction" | Medical claim; prohibited | "Helps you build better digital habits" |
| 10 | "Guaranteed focus" | Unverifiable outcome claim | "Designed to help you stay focused" |
| 11 | "100% effective" | Unverifiable | "Works across apps and websites" |
| 12 | "Blocks all distractions" | Overpromise; cannot block phone calls, etc. | "Block the apps and websites that distract you" |
| 13 | "Takes control of your device" | Implies malware-like behavior | "Puts you in control of your screen time" |
| 14 | "Hides apps" | Not what the app does; misleading | "Blocks access to selected apps during sessions" |

#### VPN Usage Disclosure (for Play Console Data Safety + Description)

```
VPN DISCLOSURE: FocusTime creates a local VPN connection on your device solely to
intercept DNS requests for the purpose of blocking websites you have selected.
This VPN operates entirely on-device. No network traffic is routed through
external servers. No browsing data is monitored, logged, or transmitted. The VPN
is active only during focus sessions and is terminated when the session ends.
```

#### Foreground Service Disclosure

```
FOREGROUND SERVICE DISCLOSURE: FocusTime uses foreground services to (1) monitor
which app is in the foreground so it can enforce app blocking, and (2) run the
local VPN for website blocking. These services display a persistent notification
showing your session countdown. Services run only during active focus sessions
and stop automatically when the session ends or the timer expires.
```

#### Overlay (SYSTEM_ALERT_WINDOW) Disclosure

```
OVERLAY DISCLOSURE: FocusTime uses the "Display Over Other Apps" permission to
show a full-screen blocking overlay when you attempt to open an app that is on
your blocklist during an active focus session. The overlay provides a "Return
Home" button and, if a master password is set, an "Emergency Override" option.
This overlay is only shown during active focus sessions.
```

#### Device Admin Disclosure

```
DEVICE ADMIN DISCLOSURE: FocusTime requests Device Administrator privileges to
help protect the active focus session. This prevents the app from being
uninstalled while a session is running. Device Admin can be deactivated at any
time through your device settings when no session is active.
```

#### QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES Disclosure

```
PACKAGE QUERY DISCLOSURE: FocusTime uses the QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES permission to

is not collected, stored beyond the blocklist, or transmitted.
```

#### Usage Access Disclosure

```
USAGE ACCESS DISCLOSURE: FocusTime uses the Usage Access permission to detect
which app is currently in the foreground. This is required to determine whether a
blocked app has been opened, so the blocking overlay can be displayed. Usage data
is checked in real time and is not stored, logged, or transmitted.
```

## 4. Creative Asset Brief

### 4.1 Visual Style Guide

| Attribute | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Style** | Minimal, intentional, serious. Not playful. Not gamified. The visual language of a professional tool, not an engagement app. |
| **Color palette** | Use the app's own colors. Primary: Focus green (#1B6D3D). Background: Off-white (#FCFDF7) for light, near-black (#1A1C19) for dark. Accent sparingly. No bright candy colors. No gradients unless very subtle. The palette should feel restrained and deliberate. |
| **Typography** | Sans-serif, geometric. Suggestions: Inter, DM Sans, or Google Sans. Headlines: Bold, 28–36pt — short, declarative statements. Body: Regular, 16–18pt. No italics in overlays. No exclamation marks in headlines. |
| **Composition** | Centered device mockups. Generous whitespace. Headline text above or beside phone. One idea per screen. Nothing decorative. No floating icons, no confetti in marketing materials. |
| **Mood** | Quiet confidence. The visual equivalent of: "This tool does not need to convince you. It does one thing, and it does it well." Not aggressive. Not fun. Intentional. |

#### Text Overlay Rules (Screenshots)

- Maximum 5 words per headline overlay
- Headline font size: large enough to read at thumbnail size (~40–48pt on 1080px wide)
- Use high-contrast text (white text on dark background or dark text on light background)
- Never cover more than 30% of the screenshot area with text
- No misleading UI elements in overlays (no fake buttons, no fake ratings)
- All text must match actual app functionality

## 5. A/B Test Plan

### Experiment 1: Short Description — Functional vs Identity

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **What to test** | Short description Option 1 (functional) vs Option 3 (identity/positioning) |
| **Variant A** | "Block apps & websites. Enforce your commitment. No fluff, no tracking." |
| **Variant B** | "Not another blocker. A boundary-setting tool for focused, deep work." |
| **Hypothesis** | Variant B's identity-first framing ("Not another blocker") will produce higher conversion among the target audience (knowledge workers willing to pay 7.99€+), even if Variant A produces more total clicks. The metric that matters is paid installs, not volume. |
| **Success metric** | Store listing conversion rate (visitors → paid installs). Run for 3 weeks or until statistical significance (95% confidence). |
| **Minimum traffic** | ~3,000 store listing visitors per variant (lower threshold acceptable for paid app — conversion rates are lower). |

### Experiment 2: Screenshot Lead — Enforcement vs Setup

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **What to test** | Leading with the active session + blocked overlay (enforcement proof) vs leading with the populated home screen (setup simplicity) |
| **Variant A** | Screenshot order: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 (lead with "The Door Is Closed" + "No Way Back In") |
| **Variant B** | Screenshot order: 3, 4, 5, 6, 1, 2, 7, 8 (lead with "Pick. Block. Commit." + "Choose What To Block") |
| **Hypothesis** | Variant A (enforcement-first) will convert better because it immediately answers the critical question: "What makes this different from Digital Wellbeing?" Leading with the locked state communicates seriousness. |
| **Success metric** | Store listing conversion rate. Run for 3 weeks minimum. |
| **Minimum traffic** | ~3,000 store listing visitors per variant. |

### Experiment 3: Title — "Deep Work" vs "Block Distractions"

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **What to test** | Title Option A (FocusTime: Deep Work Blocker) vs Title Option C (FocusTime: Block Distractions) |
| **Variant A** | "FocusTime: Deep Work Blocker" — niche identity, lower search volume, higher intent |
| **Variant B** | "FocusTime: Block Distractions" — broad search term, higher volume, more competition |
| **Hypothesis** | "Deep Work Blocker" will produce fewer impressions but a higher conversion-to-install rate and better user quality (fewer uninstalls, higher ratings) because it self-selects for the target audience. "Block Distractions" will produce more impressions but more competition and lower conversion for a paid app. |
| **Success metric** | Composite: (1) search CTR, (2) conversion rate, (3) 7-day retention. Run for 4 weeks to capture retention data. |
| **Minimum traffic** | ~5,000 search impressions per variant. |
